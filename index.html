What it actually does

Not another universal remote.

Built around the specific things a media PC needs — precise Kodi control, a real screen mirror in both directions, and a launcher that's actually yours.

🎮

Built for Kodi, precisely

Real playback position over local JSON-RPC — drag the seek bar to an exact second, jump to a timestamp, toggle shuffle and repeat. Not a guess dressed up as a remote.

📺

Screen mirror, both ways

Watch the PC's desktop live on your phone, or cast your phone's screen to the PC over Windows' own Miracast — no app installs, no extra accounts.

⬡

Make it yours

Add any site or app as a shortcut with its own icon. Export a trimmed-down version for a different room or a different person — same remote, curated launcher.

✨

Copilot & Voice Access, one tap

Always in the header, not buried in a menu. Trackpad and keyboard control for everything else the D-pad can't reach.

Setup

Three steps. No wizard.

The whole thing is one exe. No dependencies, no background installer, nothing phoning home.

1

Download & unzip

Put the folder anywhere on the media PC — MediaRemote.exe and Setup.bat just need to stay together.

2

Run Setup.bat

One UAC prompt adds the firewall rule, sets it to start with Windows, and drops a shortcut on your Desktop.

3

Open it on your phone

The console shows your PC's address. Open it in any browser — no app to install on the phone side either.

Pick your build.

Both are free, local-only, and deploy the same way. The LMC build adds a dedicated room tab for TCL TV and Sonos control.
